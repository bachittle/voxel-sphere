Voxel Sphere

A from-scratch WebGL voxel game where the world is a finite sphere instead of a flat grid — "down" is toward a molten core, "up" is out to the sky.

Direction: a Minecraft-style creative sandbox — dig through the onion, build on the curve. Roadmap in GOAL.md.

Icosphere Planet

The same Perlin world rebuilt on the icosphere's hex grid: hex-prism columns, MC textures on hexagonal caps, the 12 pentagons marked magenta, click-to-dig. The decision probe of 2026-07-02 — the hexes are more even, but square textures on square faces feel like Minecraft, so the cubed sphere is the game's canonical grid. Frozen as an archive.

Onion Depth Cutaway

Depth distortion: naive shells crowd the core; the onion (cells ∝ r²) stays cube-ish all the way down, from lava core to surface.
Decisions — Surface: cubed sphere (2.18×; the Minecraft feel beat the icosphere's 1.12× evenness) · Depth: onion, cells ∝ r² (1.15× vs 3.98× naive; shell merges are canon). Lower = more even, 1.0 = perfect.
Built from scratch, no dependencies — just WebGL + a hand-rolled mat4. Node checks: game-check.mjs, chunk-check.mjs, icosphere-check.mjs, planet-check.mjs, measure-all.mjs, depth-measure.mjs, compute-ratio.mjs. Roadmap in GOAL.md.
